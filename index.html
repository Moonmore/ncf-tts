NCF-TTS: Enhancing Flow Matching based Text-to-Speech with Neighborhood Consistency Flow

Abstract: Recent advances in diffusion and flow matching generative models have achieved remarkable performance. However, their practical use is limited by slow inference and theoretical incompatibilities with guidance methods such as classifier-free guidance (CFG). In this work, we propose a unified framework for few-step text-to-speech (TTS). First, we introduce Neighborhood Consistency Flow (NCF), a principled local transport regularizer that enforces additivity of average velocity fields, which stabilizes large-step sampling. Second, we address the fundamental incompatibility between few-step models and CFG by proposing an embedded guidance objective, which unifies conditional and unconditional supervision into the training process. Finally, we jointly optimize flow matching supervision and the NCF consistency loss, improving convergence and reducing the train and test mismatch. Experiments on multilingual TTS datasets demonstrate that NCF-TTS maintains high audio quality under few-step inference while significantly reducing computational cost.
